You drove the machine by hand

What your hands found out
  1. You moved two arrows — and every point of the picture followed.
  2. You met the matrix: just those two arrows, written down as columns of numbers.
  3. You proved order matters — rotate-then-stretch is not stretch-then-rotate.
  4. You ran machines backwards — and met the one kind that flattens space, with no way back.

Now the catch: a computer has no hands. When a game console redraws its world, nobody drags the arrows — the machine has to calculate where every point lands, from four numbers alone. Part 2 is you discovering that calculation — how to push a point through a machine, chain machines together, and finally pin down the one number that says how much a machine stretches space.
